About Me

Im Ramesh Prakash Khade, an educator, researcher, and communication specialist passionate about leveraging technology and creative design to transform learning experiences. I hold dual M.A. degrees—in Education & Technology, and in Social Work (Children & Families)—from the Tata Institute of Social Sciences (TISS), Mumbai.

Over the past decade, I have actively contributed to large-scale education innovation, teacher development, curriculum design, and digital learning initiatives. My involvement in the CLIx program across Mizoram, Rajasthan, Chhattisgarh, and Telangana helped create interactive learning resources that reached over 76,000+ students, 3,500+ teachers, and 244 teacher educators across more than 548 government schools.1

In addition, I designed textbooks for SCERT Telangana distributed to students from grades 5 to 10 in four different languages, directly impacting thousands of learners. I have also contributed to the “World of Work” program for the Delhi and Punjab governments.

My expertise lies at the intersection of print and digital design, education communication, visual storytelling, and AI-driven learning tools. I have presented research at international conferences such as T4E and IIE, focusing on AI in learning, design literacy, inclusive pedagogy, and 21st-century educational tools. I have also created and edited publications for UNESCO, UNICEF, Routledge, NORRAG, and other major educational initiatives.

Beyond research and design, I conduct professional development workshops for educators on visual storytelling, design literacy, media literacy, technology integration, and social media management. At TISS, I lead strategic communications, social media, and public outreach to amplify the impact of educational projects.

My mission is to bridge the digital divide and foster equitable learning opportunities through technology, creativity, and collaboration.
